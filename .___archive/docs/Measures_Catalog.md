# F1D Measures Catalog

This document provides a profile of the linguistic measures generated by the F1D pipeline.

## 1. MaQaUnc (Manager Q&A Uncertainty)
*   **Description**: Measures the frequency of "Uncertainty" words spoken by company executives (Managers) during the Question & Answer (Q&A) session of earnings calls.
*   **Context**: Q&A Session (Spontaneous, unscripted responses).
*   **Speaker Role**: Managers (CEO, CFO, etc.).
*   **Dictionary**: Loughran-McDonald Uncertainty (297 words like "approximate", "contingency", "depend", "fluctuate").
*   **Rationale**: Captures executives' uncertainty when forced to answer analyst questions off-the-cuff. High values may indicate low visibility or evasiveness.

## 2. MaPresUnc (Manager Presentation Uncertainty)
*   **Description**: Measures the frequency of "Uncertainty" words spoken by company executives during the Presentation (Scripted) session of earnings calls.
*   **Context**: Presentation Session (Prepared, scripted remarks).
*   **Speaker Role**: Managers.
*   **Dictionary**: Loughran-McDonald Uncertainty.
*   **Rationale**: Captures the "official" level of uncertainty the company wishes to convey. Since this text is vetted by legal/PR, high uncertainty here is a deliberate signal of risk or ambiguity.

## 3. AnaQaUnc (Analyst Q&A Uncertainty)
*   **Description**: Measures the frequency of "Uncertainty" words spoken by financial analysts during the Q&A session.
*   **Context**: Q&A Session.
*   **Speaker Role**: Analysts.
*   **Dictionary**: Loughran-McDonald Uncertainty.
*   **Rationale**: Proxies for the external information environment's opacity. If analysts are using uncertain language ("I'm confused about...", "It seems unclear..."), it suggests the firm's disclosure is hard to interpret.

## 4. EntireCallNeg (Entire Call Negativity)
*   **Description**: Measures the frequency of "Negative" words spoken by **all participants** (Managers, Analysts, Operators) across the **entire transcript** (Presentation + Q&A).
*   **Context**: Entire Call.
*   **Speaker Role**: All (Managers + Analysts).
*   **Dictionary**: Loughran-McDonald Negative (2,345 words like "loss", "decline", "failure", "adverse").
*   **Rationale**: Provides a holistic sentiment score for the event. Unlike uncertainty, negativity is often pervasive and interactive (e.g., an analyst asks a negative question, manager gives a negative answer). Measuring it globally captures the overall "tone" of the disclosure event better than isolating specific sections.
