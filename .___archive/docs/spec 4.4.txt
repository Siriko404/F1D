

### **1. The Econometric Model**

[cite_start]The estimation relies on the following Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression equation[cite: 345]:

$$UncAnsCEO_{i,t} = \alpha + \gamma_{i} \cdot CEO_{i,t} + \beta_{s} \cdot Speech_{i,t}^{s} + \beta_{k} \cdot FirmChars_{i,t}^{k} + Year_{t} + \epsilon_{i,t}$$

Where:
* **$UncAnsCEO_{i,t}$:** The dependent variable (how fuzzy the CEO's answers are).
* **$CEO_{i,t}$:** An indicator (dummy) variable for the specific CEO $i$.
* **$\gamma_{i}$:** The **CEO Fixed Effect**. This coefficient represents the CEO's personal, persistent tendency to use uncertainty words, distinct from the firm's situation.
* **$Speech_{i,t}^{s}$:** Linguistic control variables (e.g., how fuzzy the presentation was).
* **$FirmChars_{i,t}^{k}$:** Firm-level controls (financial performance).
* **$Year_{t}$:** Time fixed effects (controlling for global events like the 2008 crisis).
* **$\epsilon_{i,t}$:** The residual (unexplained) uncertainty in that specific call.

---

### **2. Calculating "Clarity" from the Model**

The regression produces a coefficient $\gamma_{i}$ for every CEO. A high $\gamma_{i}$ means the CEO uses *many* uncertainty words (they are a "fuzzy talker").

[cite_start]Because the authors want to measure **Clarity** (the opposite of uncertainty), they define the final variable as the negative of this coefficient[cite: 346]:

$$ClarityCEO_{i} = -\gamma_{i}$$

* **Result:** A higher $ClarityCEO$ score indicates a CEO who rarely uses uncertainty words in their answers, relative to the controls.
* [cite_start]**Standardization:** For the final analysis, this value is standardized to have a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1[cite: 385].

### **3. Construction of Variables**

[cite_start]Below is the detailed construction of every variable used in Equation 4, based on the text and Table A.1[cite: 1088, 1089, 1091, 1100].

#### **A. The Dependent Variable**
* **Variable:** **$UncAnsCEO$** (Uncertainty in Answers)
* **Construction:**
    1.  The authors isolate the "Answers" (Q&A) portion of the transcript.
    2.  They filter for words spoken specifically by the CEO.
    3.  [cite_start]They count the number of "uncertainty words" using the Loughran and McDonald (2011) list (297 words including *approximately, probably, maybe, could, believe*)[cite: 260].
    4.  **Formula:**
    $$UncAnsCEO = \frac{\text{Count of Uncertainty Words in Answers}}{\text{Total Words in Answers}} \times 100$$
    * [cite_start]*Note:* The average is 0.79%[cite: 283].

#### **B. The Key Linguistic Control**
* **Variable:** **$UncPreCEO$** (Uncertainty in Presentation)
* [cite_start]**Purpose:** This captures the "Firm Effect"—the uncertainty driven by the company's culture or current economic reality[cite: 364].
* **Construction:**
    1.  Isolate the "Presentation" (scripted) portion of the transcript.
    2.  Filter for words spoken by the CEO.
    3.  Count uncertainty words from the same Loughran and McDonald list.
    4.  **Formula:**
    $$UncPreCEO = \frac{\text{Count of Uncertainty Words in Presentation}}{\text{Total Words in Presentation}} \times 100$$

#### **C. Other Linguistic Controls ($Speech_{i,t}^{s}$)**
To ensure the CEO's word choice isn't just mirroring the analyst or the general mood, they control for:

* **$UncQue$** (Uncertainty in Questions)
    * [cite_start]**Construction:** The percentage of uncertainty words used by **analysts** in their questions during the Q&A[cite: 365, 1090]. This controls for the possibility that vague questions solicit vague answers.
* **$NegCall$** (Negativity)
    * [cite_start]**Construction:** The percentage of **negative words** (from the Loughran and McDonald negative word list) relative to total words spoken by all participants (CEO, CFO, analysts) during the entire call[cite: 376, 1090]. This controls for bad news, which naturally creates uncertainty.

#### **D. Firm Characteristic Controls ($FirmChars_{i,t}^{k}$)**
[cite_start]These variables control for the firm's financial performance and stock behavior at the time of the call[cite: 375, 1021, 1100].

* **$SurpDec$** (Earnings Surprise Decile)
    * **Construction:**
        1.  Calculate the raw surprise: $(\text{Actual Earnings} - \text{Consensus Forecast}) / \text{Share Price}$.
        2.  Group firms into 10 bins (deciles).
        3.  Bins range from -5 (largest negative surprise) to -1 (smallest negative), 0 (no surprise), and 1 (smallest positive) to 5 (largest positive).
        4.  The absolute value ($SurpDecAbs$) is often used to measure the *magnitude* of news, regardless of direction.
* **$EPS Growth$**
    * [cite_start]**Construction:** The percentage growth in Earnings Per Share compared to the same quarter in the previous year (year-over-year)[cite: 1094].
* **$StockRet$** (Stock Return)
    * **Construction:** The stock return (in %) over the previous quarter. [cite_start]Specifically, it measures the return from 5 days after the *previous* quarter's earnings call to 5 days before the *current* call[cite: 1107].
* **$MarketRet$** (Market Return)
    * [cite_start]**Construction:** The value-weighted market return over the same period as $StockRet$[cite: 1106].
