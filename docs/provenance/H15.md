### Interpretation

Speech uncertainty does not predict quarterly share repurchase activity. All 24 IV×spec combinations are insignificant (smallest p-value ≈ 0.16). Within-R² is extremely low (~2%), indicating that call-level uncertainty explains essentially none of the variation in repurchase decisions after controlling for firm/year FE and financial controls. This null result is informative: while uncertainty predicts CapEx (H13), leverage (H4 industry FE), and analyst dispersion (H5), it does not appear to influence repurchase timing.

## E. Design Decisions

1. **Quarterly REPO (not monthly):** Duong et al.'s baseline uses quarterly Compustat `cshopq`. Monthly timing refinements (Method 4/5) require 10-Q/10-K filings not available in our data.
2. **Call-quarter alignment:** merge_asof matches to reporting quarter; panel builder leads REPO by one fiscal quarter to get the call quarter, following Duong et al.'s convention.
3. **LPM (not logit/probit):** Linear probability model allows firm FE estimation without incidental parameters problem. Heteroscedasticity-robust clustered SEs mitigate the guaranteed heteroscedasticity of binary DVs.
4. **Year restriction 2004-2018:** cshopq is 100% NaN in 2002, 99.7% NaN in 2003 (field not reported by Compustat).
5. **8 base controls:** REPO ∉ {Lev, CashHoldings, CapexAt}, so all three enter as controls.
6. **Firm FE absorption:** 77% of firms (1,727/2,248) have within-firm variation in REPO. 248 always-repurchase and 85 never-repurchase firms are absorbed by firm FE — acceptable.
