### E2. CapexAt_lead construction (Stage 3)

- **Method:** `create_capex_lead()` in `build_h13_1_capex_panel.py` (lines 213-335)
- **Step 1:** Attach fyearq via `attach_fyearq()` (merge_asof backward). 112,855 of 112,968 calls matched (99.9%).
- **Step 2:** For each (gvkey, fyearq_int), find the call with the latest `start_date`. Result: one representative CapexAt per firm-fiscal-year.
- **Step 3:** Sort by (gvkey, fyearq_int) and shift CapexAt by -1 within gvkey.
- **Step 4:** Validate fiscal year consecutiveness: lead is nulled if next row is not exactly fyearq+1.
- **Step 5:** Merge lead back to all calls by (gvkey, fyearq_int). Zero-delta enforced (line 322).
- **Result:** 104,133 calls with valid CapexAt_lead; 8,835 calls with NaN lead (last fiscal year per firm + gap years + missing fyearq).

**VERIFIED IMPLEMENTATION ISSUE (Residual outliers):** Despite winsorization, extreme values persist:
- OCF_Volatility max = 32.40 (30 rows > 10; 1 row > 30). This is a rolling std -- extreme values plausible for firms with high OCF volatility but technically implausible as a std of a ratio. The per-fyearq winsorization may not be effective if these extremes are within the 1-99% range of their fiscal year group.
- TobinsQ max = 35.41 (415 rows > 10). Indicates growth firms with very high market-to-book.
- Lev max = 3.95 (667 rows > 1). Debt exceeding assets -- technically possible for distressed firms.
- CapexAt max = 1.44 (8 rows > 1). Capital expenditure exceeding lagged assets -- possible for rapidly growing firms but suspicious.

### Missing data policy

- **Listwise deletion** at regression stage: `prepare_regression_data()` drops rows with NaN in any required variable (DV, RHS, controls, identifiers).
- **No imputation** anywhere in the pipeline.
- **CEO uncertainty variables have ~32% missing** (CEO not identified in call parser). This creates a structural difference between CEO-spec and Manager-spec samples: CEO specs use ~54-58K obs vs Manager specs using ~74-80K obs in Main sample.
- **OCF_Volatility has 5.8% missing** (requires 3+ years of data per firm).

### Denominator protections

- CapexAt: `atq_annual_lag1 > 0` guard; zero/negative -> NaN (line 1064 of `_compustat_engine.py`)
- Size: `atq > 0` guard (line 1027)
- Lev: no guard -- dlcq.fillna(0) + dlttq.fillna(0) / atq with no atq>0 check. If atq <= 0, Lev = inf/NaN, but inf is replaced with NaN at line 1133.
- TobinsQ: `atq > 0` and `mktcap not null` guard (line 1057)
- ROA: `avg_assets > 0` guard (line 1042)
- Post-computation: all inf values replaced with NaN for all ratio columns (lines 1114-1133).

### Scaling

- No standardization of variables.
- Uncertainty measures are percentages (0-100 scale, but typically 0-3%).
- Financial controls are in natural units (log for Size, ratios for others).

## J. Known Issues / Ambiguities (Actionable)

### J1. Duplicate (gvkey, year) index and calendar-year vs fiscal-year timing mismatch

**Issue:** The panel has ~4 calls per firm-year on average (max 38), but PanelOLS is indexed on `(gvkey, year)` where `year` is the calendar year from `start_date`. `linearmodels` accepts this and treats each call as a separate observation, but the "year" FE absorbs a single annual intercept for all calls in a given calendar year while the DV (CapexAt, CapexAt_lead) and all Compustat controls are constructed based on the fiscal year (`fyearq_int`) matched via merge_asof.

**Key structural facts (red-team verified):**
- 82.2% of (gvkey, year) groups contain calls from multiple fiscal years. This occurs because merge_asof maps earnings calls to the most recent Compustat reporting date, and calls early in a calendar year may match to the prior fiscal year.
- **CapexAt and other Compustat controls DO vary within (gvkey, year) groups** because calls within the same calendar year can be matched to different fiscal years. Specifically, CapexAt varies across calls in 21,994 of 28,945 non-trivial (gvkey, year) groups (76%). This is NOT pseudo-replication in the classical sense -- there is genuine within-group variation in both the DV and controls.
- CapexAt IS constant within (gvkey, fyearq_int) groups (verified: 0 groups with varying non-null CapexAt within fiscal year).
- 24.2% of observations (27,294 rows) have calendar year != fiscal year (`year != fyearq_int`).

**Consequence:** The PanelOLS time FE absorbs calendar-year shocks, but the DV responds to fiscal-year timing. For nearly a quarter of observations, these differ. The time FE does not cleanly absorb the time-varying confounds most relevant to the DV's fiscal-year timing. Additionally, two calls from the same firm in the same calendar year may have genuinely different DVs because they straddle a fiscal year boundary, creating within-group error correlation that entity-level clustering may not fully address.

**What this is NOT:** This is not pure pseudo-replication. The earlier characterization that "only linguistic variables vary within (gvkey, year)" was factually incorrect for 82% of groups. The effective sample size is not simply the number of unique firm-years (28,975) because there is genuine variation in DVs and controls within these groups.

**Recommended fix:** Use `fyearq_int` as the PanelOLS time dimension instead of `year`. This would align the time FE with the DV and control timing, eliminate the calendar-vs-fiscal mismatch, and make the time FE properly absorb fiscal-year-level confounds. If collapsing to a single observation per group is desired for simplicity, collapse by `(gvkey, fyearq_int)`, NOT by `(gvkey, year)`, as the latter would conflate observations from different fiscal years. Alternatively, use fiscal-quarter FE for finer temporal control.

### J2. No LaTeX table generation

**Issue:** The regression runner generates `.txt` regression summaries and a markdown report, but does not call `make_accounting_table()` to produce a publication-grade LaTeX table. The import is present (line 56) but never used. Additionally, `generate_attrition_table` is imported (line 59) but never called. Other suites (H7, H1) produce `.tex` files.

**Impact:** Missing thesis-quality output artifact. Must be generated manually or the code must be extended.

**Recommended fix:** Add LaTeX table generation to `save_outputs()` or `generate_report()`. Remove or implement `generate_attrition_table`.

### J3. One-tailed test direction with mixed sign evidence

**Issue:** The hypothesis test is one-tailed (beta < 0), but many coefficients are positive. The one-tailed p-value formula correctly handles this (p_one = 1 - p_two/2 when beta > 0), but the fact that half the coefficients are positive undermines the one-tailed test premise.

**Impact:** The one-tailed test inflates apparent significance for negative coefficients while masking the lack of consistent directional evidence. A two-tailed test or a pre-registered direction would be more appropriate given the mixed evidence.

### J4. Terminology: "IV" label for uncertainty measures

**Issue:** The code comments and `run_regression()` function refer to the uncertainty measure as "IV" (line 284: `IV: {uncertainty_var}`). This is misleading -- there is no instrumental variable in this suite. The uncertainty measure is a standard RHS regressor.

**Impact:** Documentation confusion; a referee might expect an IV/2SLS setup. This is a labeling issue only.

**Recommended fix:** Replace "IV" with "key RHS" or "treatment variable" in print statements and comments.

### J5. Stale output artifact from prior run

**Issue:** The directory `outputs/econometric/h13_1_capex/2026-03-06_174710/` contains outputs from an earlier code version with a different schema: only 3 regressions, column `primary_iv` instead of `uncertainty_var`, and no `dependent_var` column. The `get_latest_output_dir` function mitigates this by selecting the most recent timestamp directory, but the stale artifacts remain.

**Impact:** A replicator manually browsing the output directories could load the March 6 run's `model_diagnostics.csv` and find a completely different result set. Low risk in practice but a reproducibility hazard.

**Recommended fix:** Delete or archive the `2026-03-06_174710` output directory.

### J6. Run manifest input path is incorrect

**Issue:** Line 604 of `run_h13_1_capex.py` constructs the panel input path as `root / "outputs" / "variables" / "h13_1_capex" / "latest" / "h13_1_capex_panel.parquet"`, but no `latest` symlink or directory exists. The actual panel is loaded via `get_latest_output_dir` (lines 165-168), which picks the most recent timestamp directory. Because the recorded path does not exist, the manifest's input hash is null.

**Impact:** The `run_manifest.json` cannot be used to verify input provenance. The provenance chain is more broken than a simple "null hash" suggests -- the root cause is a wrong path.

**Recommended fix:** Fix line 604 to use the actual resolved `panel_dir` path from `get_latest_output_dir` rather than the hardcoded `latest` path.

## N. Final Readiness Statement

**Can this suite be reproduced end-to-end from code alone?**
Mostly yes. Both Stage 3 (panel build) and Stage 4 (regression) scripts are self-contained, load data from raw inputs and upstream artifacts, and produce all outputs deterministically. No manual steps required. However, the run manifest's input path is incorrect (references a non-existent `latest` directory), breaking the provenance chain at the manifest level, and a stale output directory from a prior run could confuse replication attempts.

**Can a committee/referee understand exactly how the variables are built?**
Yes. Variable construction is traceable through the builder pattern to raw Compustat fields. The CapexAt_lead construction logic is well-documented in code comments and this provenance document. The fiscal-year-based lead with consecutiveness validation is a sound approach. Note: TobinsQ uses the market-debt approach `(cshoq*prccq + debt_book) / atq`, not a ceqq-based formula.

**Are the implemented results trustworthy for descriptive inference?**
Partially. The regression mechanics are correct, but the calendar-year time FE vs fiscal-year DV timing mismatch means the time FE does not cleanly absorb the confounds most relevant to the DV. After fixing the time index to fiscal year and applying multiple-testing correction, the 3/24 significant specs would likely fall to 0-1. The descriptive evidence for an uncertainty-CapEx link is weak.

**Are they trustworthy for causal inference?**
No. The contemporaneous specs suffer from reverse causality. The forward-looking specs (CapexAt_lead) partially address timing but lack identification beyond firm and year FE. No placebo tests, no pre-trend analysis, no instrumental variables, no robustness battery. The evidence does not support causal claims.

**What is the single biggest threat to credibility?**
Multiple testing without correction. Reporting 3/24 significant specs as evidence for H13.1 when the expected false-positive count under the null is 1.2 is the most likely point of referee attack. The calendar-year vs fiscal-year timing mismatch (L-15) is a close second, as it undermines the validity of the time FE structure.

**What is the single highest-value next fix?**
Switch the PanelOLS time dimension from `year` (calendar year) to `fyearq_int` (fiscal year). This single change would (a) align the time FE with the DV and control timing, (b) eliminate the 24.2% timing mismatch, (c) resolve the within-group error correlation from fiscal-year straddling, and (d) make the time FE properly absorb fiscal-year-level confounds. This addresses L-15, L-16, and partially L-05 simultaneously.
