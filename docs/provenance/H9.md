## II. Research Question and Current Hypotheses

**Research Question:** Does CEO speech clarity affect the probability of receiving a takeover bid? Do clearer CEOs face lower takeover hazard, particularly for uninvited (hostile/unsolicited) bids?

**Hypotheses:**
- **H9-A:** beta(Clarity) < 0 — clearer CEOs have lower overall takeover hazard
- **H9-B:** beta(Clarity, uninvited) < beta(Clarity, friendly) — clarity reduces hostile takeover hazard more than friendly

### Cause-Specific Competing Risks

- **Uninvited models:** Only Hostile+Unsolicited bids are events. Friendly and Unknown bids are censored.
- **Friendly models:** Only Friendly+Neutral bids are events. Uninvited and Unknown bids are censored.
- **Unknown type handling:** Firms with Takeover=1 but Takeover_Type='Unknown' are censored in both cause-specific models. This is correct competing-risks practice.

## IX. Known Limitations / Open Issues

1. **ClarityManager absent:** Manager clarity score does not exist in the repo. Only CEO clarity score is available as a primary style construct. Manager_Clarity_Residual is available as secondary.

2. **ClarityCEO coverage:** 59.7% of intervals have ClarityCEO (64,217/107,644). This is expected — ClarityCEO requires sufficient CEO tenure for reliable fixed-effect estimation.

3. **LaTeX table formatting:** The `takeover_table.tex` output has known alignment issues (standard errors separated from coefficients for some variants). The CSV output (`hazard_ratios.csv`) is authoritative.

4. **Robust SEs:** Not available in lifelines 0.30.0 for `CoxTimeVaryingFitter` (`robust=True` raises `NotImplementedError`). Would need R's `survival` package or a lifelines upgrade.

5. **PH Assumption Test:** `check_assumptions()` not compatible with `CoxTimeVaryingFitter` (raises `AttributeError`). Would need manual Schoenfeld residual computation or R.

6. **Uninvited strata sparsity:** Only 40 Uninvited event firms in the complete-case sample across 17 years / 10 industries. Year-stratified Uninvited model has 14 strata with <5 events; industry-stratified has 7. Models converge but estimates may be unstable. Residual variants have even fewer Uninvited events (CEO_Residual: ~36), producing heavier sparsity warnings in stratified models — expected behavior, script handles gracefully (skips if <5 total events).
