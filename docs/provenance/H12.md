## G. Outliers, Missing Data, Scaling, and Model-Specific Data Rules

### G1) Winsorization

| Variable type | Method | Thresholds | Where applied | Code reference |
|--------------|--------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Compustat financial controls + DivIntensity | By-year (within fyearq) | 1%/99% | CompustatEngine, before merge to manifest | `_compustat_engine.py:1138-1161`, `_winsorize_by_year()` at line 436 |
| Linguistic percentage columns | By-year (within calendar year) | 0%/99% (upper-only) | LinguisticEngine, after loading all years | `_linguistic_engine.py:255-258` |
| Firm-year averaged uncertainty | NOT re-winsorized | -- | -- | Averaging already compresses distribution; no post-aggregation winsor |

**REFEREE CONCERN:** Winsorization is applied at the engine level (quarterly Compustat / call-level linguistic), but DivIntensity is winsorized BEFORE the Q4 annual extraction. Since `_compute_and_winsorize` operates on all quarterly rows, the by-year winsorization clips the quarterly-level DivIntensity distribution, not the firm-year panel distribution. After aggregation to firm-year (taking "last" per firm-year), the final panel distribution may differ from what by-year 1%/99% would produce on the firm-year panel itself. This is acceptable but should be documented.

### G2) Missing data policy

- **Listwise deletion** at regression time: `run_regression()` drops rows with NaN in any of DV + uncertainty measure + controls + identifiers (`dropna(subset=required_avail)`, line 295).
- **Inf replacement:** `np.inf` and `-np.inf` replaced with NaN before dropna (line 295).
- **No imputation** for any variable.
- DivIntensity: `dvy` NaN filled to 0 before division (line 1082), treating missing dividends as zero dividends.

### G3) Denominator protections

- DivIntensity: NaN when `atq_{t-1} <= 0` (line 1080-1084).
- Size: NaN when `atq <= 0`.
- All ratio variables in CompustatEngine: inf replaced with NaN post-computation (lines 1114-1133).

### G4) Scaling

- `Size = ln(atq)` -- log transformation.
- All other variables: level (no log, no standardization).
- Uncertainty measures: percentage scale (0-100 nominally, though effectively 0-~5% for most).

## K. Corporate Finance Thesis Referee Assessment

### K1) Bottom-line referee verdict

This suite tests whether CEO/manager uncertainty language in earnings calls predicts lower dividend intensity. The implementation is technically clean: correct panel construction, proper firm + year FE, firm-clustered SEs, lead DV for predictive timing. The hypothesis is rejected across all 24 completed specifications in the predicted direction -- no coefficient is negative and statistically significant. However, the result pattern is NOT a uniform null: **8 of 12 Finance specifications show statistically significant POSITIVE coefficients (two-tailed p < 0.05)**, indicating that uncertainty language is associated with HIGHER dividend intensity in financial firms. This Main-vs-Finance divergence is a substantive finding that must be reported and discussed. More fundamentally, modeling dividend intensity with OLS when 45% of observations are exactly zero is an econometric concern that undermines the validity of linear coefficient interpretation. The within-R-squared values are extremely low (0.011-0.036), indicating that the uncertainty measures explain essentially none of the within-firm variation in dividend intensity after absorbing firm and year effects.

**Overall status: SALVAGEABLE WITH MAJOR REVISIONS**

The implementation currently supports: **descriptive association only** (and the association is null in the Main sample, significantly positive in the Finance sample).

The write-up would be overselling the implemented evidence if it claims any predictive or causal relationship. If the thesis presents H12 as a universal null result, it would be **factually misleading** -- the Finance sample shows a robust opposite-direction effect. The thesis must present the heterogeneous pattern: null in non-financial firms, significantly positive in financial firms.

### K6) Interpretation and claims discipline

- **Causal verbs:** NOT justified. The one-tailed test framing ("higher uncertainty language leads to lower dividend intensity") implies causality, but the identification strategy (firm + year FE, no instrument) cannot establish causation. This is descriptive association at best.
- **Null result characterization -- CORRECTED:** The result is NOT a universal null. The Main sample (12 specs) shows genuinely null results with no significant coefficients in either direction. The Finance sample (12 specs) shows a robust pattern of significant POSITIVE coefficients (8/12 at p_two < 0.05), indicating that uncertainty is associated with HIGHER dividend intensity in financial firms -- the opposite of H12.
- **Main vs Finance divergence -- CRITICAL DISCUSSION REQUIRED:** A thesis presenting "null results across all specifications" would be factually misleading. The divergence between Main (null) and Finance (significantly positive) is econometrically important and must be explicitly discussed. Possible explanations include:
  - **Different payout norms:** Financial firms (banks, insurance) face regulatory capital requirements that interact with dividend policy differently than non-financial firms.
  - **Regulatory constraints:** Financial institutions may increase dividends to signal stability during periods of uncertainty, opposite to precautionary motives in non-financial firms.
  - **Agency dynamics:** Different governance and monitoring structures in financial firms.
  - **Sample composition:** Finance sample is smaller (3,152-4,928 obs) and may have different within-firm variation patterns.
  - A referee would immediately ask why the effect reverses between Main and Finance; an interaction model (uncertainty x financial_sector) would be informative.
- **Mechanism claims:** No mechanism channel is tested (e.g., information asymmetry, agency cost, signaling).
- **Economic magnitudes:** Not interpretable for Main (null coefficients). For Finance, the positive coefficients are small but statistically significant; economic magnitude interpretation requires careful attention to the zero-inflation issue.
- **Specification-mining risk:** Low for the H12 directional claim -- all 24 specs are null in the predicted direction. However, the Finance positive results emerge across 8/12 specs and appear robust to measure choice, suggesting this is not a spurious finding.
- **P-values and star inconsistency:** One-tailed test for H12 (beta1 < 0) is correctly implemented at line 351-354. **However, the LaTeX table uses one-tailed p-values (`beta1_p_one`) for beta1 significance stars (line 491) but two-tailed p-values (`model.pvalues`) for control coefficient stars (line 503). This creates an inconsistent star notation within the same table -- a reader would likely interpret all stars using the same threshold basis.** The table note partially discloses this but it is insufficient.

## N. Final Readiness Statement

- **Can this suite be reproduced end-to-end from code alone?** PARTIAL. Requires pre-existing Stage 1 manifest, Stage 2 linguistic variables, and raw Compustat data. Given those, the panel build and regression are deterministic and reproducible, subject to unpinned `linearmodels` version. The `get_latest_output_dir()` function uses timestamp-sorted directory names (not a file-count heuristic) and is robust.

- **Can a committee/referee understand exactly how the variables are built?** YES, with effort. The variable construction chain is traceable from raw Compustat fields through the engine to the panel builder to the regression. However, docstring/code mismatches on controls (4 documented vs 7 actual) and the builder docstring error (contemporaneous vs lagged atq) would confuse a careful reader.

- **Are the implemented results trustworthy for descriptive inference?** PARTIALLY. The Main sample null result (uncertainty does not predict dividend intensity) is credible as a descriptive finding. The Finance sample positive result (uncertainty associated with higher dividend intensity) is also a credible descriptive finding, but requires explanation and is subject to the zero-inflation caveat. Missing payout-specific controls weaken both.

- **Are they trustworthy for causal inference?** NO. No IV, no exogenous variation, no mechanism test. Firm + year FE with firm-clustered SEs is a descriptive panel design, not a causal identification strategy.

- **What is the single biggest threat to credibility?** TWO co-equal threats: (1) The zero-inflated dependent variable (45.4% exact zeros) modeled with continuous OLS, which makes coefficient interpretation unreliable. (2) The unreported Finance significant positive results -- presenting H12 as a "universal null" when 8/12 Finance specifications are significantly positive in the opposite direction would be a material mischaracterization of the evidence.

- **What is the single highest-value next fix?** Correct the result narrative immediately: report the Finance two-tailed significance, add explicit discussion of the Main-vs-Finance divergence, and reframe H12 from "universal null" to "null in non-financial firms; significantly positive in financial firms." This is a low-effort, high-credibility fix that must precede any econometric improvements.
