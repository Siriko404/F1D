**Suite ID:** H0.3

---

## A. Red-Team Bottom Line

The first-layer audit is a thorough, well-structured document that correctly identifies the suite's core mechanics, variable definitions, and most material concerns. Its main weakness is a handful of factual errors (merge direction stated incorrectly in one section, a false positive on the singleton concern), several missed data-construction details in IBES/linguistic processing, and an incomplete treatment of the standardization asymmetry between financial and linguistic controls. Despite these issues, the first audit would not materially mislead a thesis committee — its verdict and severity rankings are defensible, and no thesis-killing issue was missed by either the implementation or the first audit.

**Overall grade for the first audit: PARTIALLY RELIABLE**

**Suite as implemented: THESIS-STANDARD AS IMPLEMENTED** (for its intended purpose as a robustness check)

**Risk characterization:** The first audit **mixed both** — it overstated the singleton risk (false positive) while understating the standardization asymmetry and IBES filtering details (omissions).

## N. What a Committee / Referee Would Still Not Know if They Read Only the First Audit

1. **The Compustat merge direction is stated incorrectly in section E2** — a referee reading E2 alone would believe Compustat data from the FUTURE of the call is used, which is the opposite of what the code does.

2. **Three linguistic controls are in raw percentage units while ten financial controls are in standard-deviation units** — a referee comparing coefficient magnitudes across all controls in Table 4.1.2 would be comparing apples and oranges without realizing it.

3. **The singleton concern is moot** — a referee might worry about FE singletons based on K3, but the min-calls ≥ 5 filter is applied at the right stage to prevent this.

4. **The IBES SurpDec missingness has at least 5 contributing filters**, not just the 180-day tolerance and FPEDATS ±45-day filter documented — though the additional filters are standard practice and unlikely to change conclusions.

5. **The attrition artifact produced by the code misrepresents the actual filter ordering** — though the first audit's text correctly describes the ordering, the generated CSV/TeX files are misleading.
