## 1. Suite Overview

H7 tests whether speech uncertainty during earnings calls predicts a measurable increase in stock illiquidity in the trading days immediately following the call, relative to the trading days immediately preceding it. The dependent variable is the change in the Amihud (2002) illiquidity measure around the call event window.

The suite consists of two components:

1. **Primary estimation** (`run_h7_illiquidity.py`): Four PanelOLS regressions testing the effect of four simultaneously-entered uncertainty measures on delta_amihud, varying fixed-effects structure (Industry vs. Firm) and control sets (Base vs. Extended).

2. **Selection supplement** (`ceo_presence_probit.py`): A probit model characterising the determinants of CEO participation in the Q&A segment. Approximately 29.6% of Main-sample calls lack CEO Q&A participation, making CEO_QA_Uncertainty_pct structurally missing for those observations. This probit documents that CEO absence is non-random (correlated with firm size, leverage, and volatility), which is material because the complete-case filter used in the primary estimation systematically excludes CEO-absent calls.

### CEO Presence Probit (Selection Supplement)

**Script:** `src/f1d/econometric/ceo_presence_probit.py`
**Input:** H7 panel (same parquet as primary estimation)
**DV:** `CEO_present_QA` = 1 if `CEO_QA_Uncertainty_pct` is non-null (i.e., CEO spoke during Q&A)
**Model:** Probit: `CEO_present_QA ~ Size + BookLev + ROA + TobinsQ + Volatility + C(year)`
**Estimator:** `statsmodels.formula.api.probit`, Newton method, maxiter=200
**Outputs:** `ceo_presence_probit_summary.txt`, `ceo_presence_marginal_effects.csv`
**Purpose:** Thesis disclosure of selection mechanism. CEO absence is concentrated among smaller, more volatile firms. The complete-case filter in the primary estimation drops all CEO-absent calls, producing a subsample that overrepresents larger, less volatile firms. No Heckman correction or inverse-probability weighting is applied; the probit results are disclosed as a limitation.

## 6. Variable Dictionary

### 6.1 Dependent Variable

| Variable | Column | Formula | Source | Winsorized | Notes |
|----------|--------|---------|--------|------------|-------|
| Delta Amihud | `delta_amihud` | `mean(daily_illiq[post_window]) - mean(daily_illiq[pre_window])` | CRSP DSF via AmihudChangeBuilder | **NO** | See Section 12 (Known Limitations). |

**DV construction provenance (full chain):**

1. **Raw inputs:** CRSP DSF daily: RET (holding-period return), VOL (share volume), PRC (closing price, forced to |PRC| at load). Source: `inputs/CRSP_DSF/CRSP_DSF_{year}_Q{q}.parquet`. Loaded via `CRSPEngine.get_raw_daily_data()` (`_crsp_engine.py` L457-506), which calls `_load_crsp_years()` (L45-99). At load time, all columns are renamed to uppercase (L65-66), PRC is forced to absolute value (`crsp["PRC"] = crsp["PRC"].abs()`, L92), PERMNO is coerced to numeric and NaN-PERMNO rows are dropped (L95-97, CRITICAL-4 fix).
2. **PERMNO linkage:** gvkey -> LPERMNO via date-bounded CCM join. CCM loaded from `inputs/CRSPCompustat_CCM/CRSPCompustat_CCM.parquet`. Date filter: `linkdt <= start_date <= linkenddt` (CRITICAL-3 fix). Missing `linkenddt` filled with `2099-12-31` (active link). Disambiguation: `groupby("file_name")["LPERMNO"].last()` after sorting by linkdt ascending -- selects most recent valid link. Code: `AmihudChangeBuilder._build_permno_map()`.
3. **Daily illiquidity:** `daily_illiq = |RET| / (VOL * |PRC|) * 1e6` (`amihud_change.py` L304-306). Zero dollar volume is replaced with NaN to avoid division by zero. Inf values are replaced with NaN (L308).
4. **Reference date:** Last trading day on or before `start_date` (the call date). Determined by filtering CRSP dates where `date <= start_date`, then taking the max per call (L314-318).
5. **Call-day exclusion:** `days_from_ref == 0` is excluded from both windows. This is conservative -- avoids ambiguity about whether the call occurred before or after market close.
6. **Window assignment:** Trading-day rank positions. Pre-window: rank by date descending (most recent = rank 1, i.e., day -1). Post-window: rank by date ascending (nearest = rank 1, i.e., day +1). Window size `w = 3` trading days (configurable).
7. **Aggregation:** `pre_call_amihud = mean(daily_illiq)` over pre-window days; `post_call_amihud = mean(daily_illiq)` over post-window days.
8. **Minimum valid days:** Both windows require at least `min_pre = max(1, w-1) = 2` and `min_post = max(1, w-1) = 2` valid (non-NaN) trading days. Calls failing this filter get NaN for both delta_amihud and pre_call_amihud (L363-368).
9. **Delta:** `delta_amihud = post_call_amihud - pre_call_amihud` (L360).
10. **Scaling:** The 1e6 multiplier in step 3 follows Amihud (2002) convention for readability. The resulting units are "absolute return per million dollars of trading volume."
11. **No winsorization:** Neither delta_amihud nor pre_call_amihud is winsorized at any point in the pipeline. `AmihudChangeBuilder` calls `CRSPEngine.get_raw_daily_data()` (not `get_data()`), which returns raw daily CRSP data WITHOUT the per-year winsorization that `get_data()` applies. `build_h7_illiquidity_panel.py` contains zero references to any winsorization function. See Section 12.

**Textual IV construction provenance (full chain):**

The four uncertainty IVs share an identical NLP pipeline; the only variation is the speaker filter (CEO vs. Manager) and the section filter (QA vs. Pres). The pipeline runs in three stages:

**Stage 2.1 -- Tokenization and word counting** (`src/f1d/text/tokenize_transcripts.py`):

1. **Dictionary load:** The Loughran-McDonald Master Dictionary (`inputs/Loughran-McDonald_MasterDictionary_1993-2024.csv`) is loaded. Each word is classified into categories (Positive, Negative, Uncertainty, Litigious, Constraining, Strong_Modal, Weak_Modal). Words may belong to multiple categories (overlap is documented in the run report). The vocabulary list is the union of all categorised words.
2. **Transcript load:** Raw speaker-level transcript data is loaded from `inputs/Earnings_Calls_Transcripts/speaker_data_{year}.parquet`. Each row represents one speaker turn with columns: `file_name`, `speaker_text`, `speaker_number`, `context` (values: `"qa"`, `"pres"`), `role`, `employer`, `speaker_name`. Files are loaded year-by-year and filtered to manifest `file_name` values immediately to control memory.
3. **Tokenization:** `sklearn.feature_extraction.text.CountVectorizer` is initialised with the LM vocabulary list and token pattern `r"(?u)\b[a-zA-Z]+\b"` (alphabetic tokens only, no numbers/punctuation). Speaker text is uppercased before tokenization (`str.upper()`). The vectorizer produces a sparse document-term matrix `X` where each row is one speaker turn and each column is one LM dictionary word.
4. **Category counting:** For each LM category (Uncertainty, Negative, Positive, etc.), the word indices belonging to that category are identified in the feature map. Category counts per speaker turn are computed as column sums of the sparse matrix slice: `X[:, indices].sum(axis=1)`. Output column: `{category}_count` (e.g., `Uncertainty_count`).
5. **Total token counting:** Total tokens per speaker turn are counted independently using the regex `r"(?u)\b[a-zA-Z]+\b"` applied to the uppercased text. Output column: `total_tokens`. This denominator counts ALL alphabetic tokens, not just LM vocabulary words.
6. **Output:** Year-partitioned parquet files `linguistic_counts_{year}.parquet` with columns: `file_name`, `speaker_number`, `context`, `role`, `employer`, `speaker_name`, `{category}_count` (one per LM category), `total_tokens`.

**Stage 2.2 -- Variable construction** (`src/f1d/text/build_linguistic_variables.py`):

7. **Speaker flagging:** Each speaker turn is classified using the following rules:
   - `is_analyst`: `role` contains "analyst" (case-insensitive).
   - `is_operator`: `role` contains "operator" (case-insensitive).
   - `is_manager`: NOT analyst AND NOT operator AND (role matches manager keyword pattern from `inputs/Manager_roles/managerial_roles_extracted.txt` OR employer matches company name `conm` from manifest).
   - `is_ceo`: speaker name matches CEO name (exact or last-name match) from manifest fields `ceo_name_lower`, `prev_ceo_name_lower`, `ceo_last`, `prev_ceo_last`. CEO names are sourced from Execucomp via Stage 1 `build_tenure_map.py`.
8. **Section filtering:** Context masks partition speaker turns: `QA` = `context == "qa"`, `Pres` = `context == "pres"`, `All` = `context.isin(["qa", "pres"])`.
9. **Aggregation (ratio-of-sums):** For each (speaker group, section) combination, speaker turns matching both masks are selected, then grouped by `file_name`. Within each call, category counts and `total_tokens` are summed across all qualifying speaker turns. The percentage is computed as: `pct = (sum(category_count) / sum(total_tokens)) * 100.0`. Zero total tokens produces NaN (via `replace(0, np.nan)` before division). This is a ratio-of-sums, NOT a mean-of-ratios -- it weights longer speaker turns more heavily, which is the standard approach for document-level linguistic measures.
10. **Column naming:** Output column = `{Speaker}_{Section}_{Category}_pct`, e.g., `CEO_QA_Uncertainty_pct`. Output is one row per `file_name` per (speaker, section) combination.
11. **Year partitioning:** Output saved as `linguistic_variables_{year}.parquet` in `outputs/2_Textual_Analysis/2.2_Variables/{timestamp}/`.

**Stage 3 -- Engine-level winsorization** (`src/f1d/shared/variables/_linguistic_engine.py`):

12. **Load:** `LinguisticEngine.get_data()` loads all `linguistic_variables_{year}.parquet` files, concatenates them, and adds a `year` column from the parquet filename.
13. **Winsorization:** All `_pct` columns are winsorized at **0%/99% (upper-only) per year** via `winsorize_by_year(combined, existing_pct_cols, year_col="year", lower=0.0, upper=0.99, min_obs=10)`. This is upper-only winsorization: the lower bound is 0% (no left clipping -- linguistic percentages have a natural floor at zero), the upper bound clips at the 99th percentile within each calendar year. Years with fewer than 10 valid observations are skipped. Inf values are converted to NaN before clipping.
14. **Caching:** The winsorized DataFrame is cached as a module-level singleton; all individual textual builders (CEOQAUncertaintyBuilder, etc.) query this cache and extract their single column.

**Structural missingness:**

- `CEO_QA_Uncertainty_pct` is NaN when the CEO does not participate in Q&A (~29.6% of Main calls). This is structural, not a data quality issue -- if the CEO spoke zero words in Q&A, no uncertainty percentage can be computed.
- `CEO_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` is NaN when the CEO does not participate in the presentation segment.
- `Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct` and `Manager_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` are NaN only when no managers spoke in the relevant section (rare).
- The complete-case filter in the runner drops all observations where any IV is NaN.

**Compustat control construction provenance (full chain):**

All Compustat controls share a common pipeline through `CompustatEngine` (`_compustat_engine.py`). The engine is a module-level singleton: it loads once and caches the result for all builders in the process.

**Common Compustat pipeline (steps shared by all controls):**

1. **Raw load:** `CompustatEngine.get_data()` loads `inputs/comp_na_daily_all/comp_na_daily_all.parquet` with all columns listed in `REQUIRED_COMPUSTAT_COLS` (L142-181). The `gvkey` column is zero-padded to 6 digits. `datadate` is converted to datetime. All numeric columns (except gvkey, datadate, fyearq, fqtr, sic) are coerced to float64 via `pd.to_numeric(errors="coerce")`.
2. **Deduplication:** Duplicate `(gvkey, datadate)` rows are dropped, keeping the last row (most recent restatement). This is CRITICAL-2 fix: ensures deterministic merge_asof behaviour.
3. **Computation:** `_compute_and_winsorize()` computes all control variables on the full quarterly panel (L1027-1203).
4. **Inf replacement:** After all ratio computations, inf/-inf values in ratio columns are replaced with NaN (L1151-1172, MINOR-9 fix).
5. **Winsorization:** All continuous Compustat controls are winsorized at 1%/99% within each fiscal year (`fyearq`) via `_winsorize_by_year()` (L1194-1201). Skipped variables: DividendPayer (binary), InvestmentResidual/CashFlow/SalesGrowth (already winsorized internally), REPO (binary), fqtr (identifier). Minimum 10 observations per year-group required.
6. **Manifest matching:** `CompustatEngine.match_to_manifest()` merges computed controls to the call manifest via `pd.merge_asof()` on `(gvkey, datadate <= start_date)`, direction backward (L1287-1294). Each call gets the most recent Compustat quarterly observation on or before the call date.

## 9. Attrition Table

The runner generates an attrition table at runtime (`run_h7_illiquidity.py` L429-434) with the following stages. Exact counts depend on the data vintage; the structure is:

| Stage | Description | Code reference |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Full panel | Total rows from panel parquet | L392 (`full_panel_n`) |
| Main sample | After excluding FF12 codes 8 (Utility) and 11 (Finance) | L393-394 (`main_panel_n`) |
| delta_amihud non-null | Calls where DV was successfully computed from CRSP | L432 |
| Complete-case + min-calls (col 1) | After requiring all IVs, controls, DV non-null AND firm has >= 5 such calls | L433 (`first_meta.n_obs`) |

**Key attrition drivers:**
- **CEO absence (~29.6% of Main calls):** CEO_QA_Uncertainty_pct is NaN when CEO does not participate in Q&A. The complete-case filter drops all such calls. This is the largest single source of attrition after the Main-sample filter.
- **CRSP linkage failures:** Calls where gvkey cannot be mapped to a valid PERMNO via date-bounded CCM linkage produce NaN delta_amihud.
- **Insufficient trading days:** Calls where either the pre or post window has fewer than 2 valid daily_illiq observations produce NaN delta_amihud.
- **Compustat missingness:** Any control with NaN (Size, ROA, etc.) triggers row exclusion under complete cases.
- **Min-calls filter:** Firms with fewer than 5 complete-case calls are dropped entirely. This ensures sufficient within-firm variation for firm-FE estimation.

**Attrition granularity concern (RT2-4):** The attrition table jumps directly from "delta_amihud non-null" to "Complete-case + min-calls (col 1)" without separating: (a) complete-case before min-calls, (b) min-calls filter separately, and (c) breakdown of missingness by variable (i.e., which IV or control drives the most attrition). Finer-grained reporting would improve transparency.

**Note:** Exact sample sizes are not available in this document because they depend on the specific data vintage used. The runner prints all attrition counts to stdout and saves them to `attrition_table.csv`. These should be recorded from the final production run and appended here.

## 11. Identification & Inference Assessment

### 11.1 Identification Strategy

H7 identifies the effect of speech uncertainty on post-call illiquidity through within-firm (firm FE specs) or within-industry (industry FE specs) variation in uncertainty measures, after conditioning on financial controls, a lagged-DV control (pre_call_amihud), and fiscal-year fixed effects. There is no instrument, no natural experiment, and no regression discontinuity. The identifying assumption is that, conditional on the controls and fixed effects, the remaining variation in uncertainty measures is exogenous to contemporaneous illiquidity shocks.

### 11.2 Threats to Identification

| Threat | Severity | Discussion |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Reverse causality | Moderate | Firms facing illiquid markets may adopt more hedging language, producing simultaneity. The event-window design (pre/post call) partially addresses this since the call occurs at a specific point, but information leakage before the call could still confound. |
| Omitted variables | Moderate | Uncertainty and illiquidity may co-move due to common information shocks (earnings surprises, macro events) not fully absorbed by fiscal-year FE. |
| Multicollinearity | Moderate | 4 correlated uncertainty IVs enter simultaneously. CEO and Manager QA/Pres measures are mechanically correlated (CEO is a subset of Manager). This inflates standard errors and reduces power. No VIF or condition-number diagnostics are computed. |
| Selection on CEO presence | Material | ~29.6% of Main calls lack CEO Q&A participation. Complete-case analysis drops these calls, creating a non-random subsample biased toward larger, less volatile firms. No Heckman correction is applied. See probit supplement. |
| Functional form | Material | Raw delta_amihud has heavy tails. Without winsorization (see Section 12), OLS may be dominated by outliers, reducing power and potentially distorting coefficients. |

### 11.3 Pre-call Control (Lagged DV)

`pre_call_amihud` serves as a lagged-DV control, absorbing baseline firm-level illiquidity not captured by firm FE alone. This is appropriate for the event-study design and parallels H14's use of `PreCallSpread`.

### 11.4 Clustering

Firm-clustered standard errors are appropriate for call-level data with repeated firm observations. They account for within-firm correlation in residuals across calls.

## 12. Known Limitations & Unresolved Issues

### 12.1 CRITICAL: delta_amihud and pre_call_amihud Are Not Winsorized

**Status:** OPEN -- must be resolved before thesis submission.

The dependent variable (`delta_amihud`) and its lagged control (`pre_call_amihud`) are not winsorized at any point in the pipeline. This has been verified by exhaustive search:

- `build_h7_illiquidity_panel.py`: zero references to any winsorization function.
- `amihud_change.py` (AmihudChangeBuilder): zero references to any winsorization function.
- `AmihudChangeBuilder.build()` calls `CRSPEngine.get_raw_daily_data()` (L75), which returns raw CRSP daily data WITHOUT applying the per-year winsorization that `CRSPEngine.get_data()` applies to StockRet, MarketRet, Volatility, and amihud_illiq.

**Why this matters:** The Amihud (2002) illiquidity measure is notoriously right-skewed. The formula `|RET| / dollar_volume * 1e6` produces extreme outliers when dollar volume is small. Without winsorization:
1. OLS coefficients may be dominated by a few extreme observations.
2. Standard errors are inflated, reducing statistical power.
3. The null results reported for H7 may be an artifact of outlier-driven variance inflation rather than a true null effect.

**Comparability failure with H14:** The analogous suite H14 (bid-ask spread) explicitly winsorizes its event-window DV (DSPREAD) and lagged control (PreCallSpread) using `winsorize_pooled()` at 1%/99% in `build_h14_bidask_spread_panel.py` L200-203. H7 does not.

**CRITICAL: LaTeX table note is FALSE (RT2-3).** The LaTeX table note at `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L339 states: "Variables winsorized at 1%/99% by year at engine level." This is TRUE for Compustat controls (Size, BookLev, ROA, TobinsQ, CapexAt, OCF_Volatility) and CRSP Volatility, but **FALSE for delta_amihud and pre_call_amihud**. Neither variable is winsorized anywhere in the pipeline (confirmed by V17, V18, V25). The note as written is factually incorrect and must be corrected before thesis submission.

**Recommended fix:** Add `winsorize_pooled(panel, ["delta_amihud", "pre_call_amihud"])` to `build_h7_illiquidity_panel.py`, matching the H14 pattern. Correct the LaTeX table note. Re-run all 4 regressions.

### 12.2 No Log-Transform or Alternative DV Specification

The standard practice in the literature (Amihud 2002, Chordia et al. 2001) is to either (a) log-transform the Amihud measure, (b) winsorize aggressively, or (c) both. H7 does neither. A `log(1 + delta_amihud)` or rank-transform robustness check would strengthen inference.

### 12.3 No VIF/Multicollinearity Diagnostics

With 4 correlated uncertainty measures entering simultaneously, multicollinearity is a concern. No VIF or condition-number diagnostics are computed or reported. This may contribute to the null results by inflating standard errors.

### 12.4 Panel Index Non-Uniqueness

The panel index `(gvkey, fyearq_int)` has duplicate entries because multiple calls can occur per firm per fiscal year. While PanelOLS handles this correctly, the thesis methodology section should disclose that the unit of observation is the individual earnings call, not the firm-year.

### 12.5 Panel Builder Docstring Error

`build_h7_illiquidity_panel.py` L18 states `DV: amihud_illiq (contemporaneous Amihud illiquidity measure)`. The actual DV is `delta_amihud` (post-call minus pre-call change), not the level measure. This is a documentation-only issue with no code impact.

### 12.6 CRSP VOL Units Assumption

The daily_illiq formula assumes CRSP DSF VOL is in actual shares. This is correct for the modern CRSP DSF period (post-2001), but VOL was reported in 100s of shares for some earlier periods. If the sample extends before 2001, a systematic scaling error could result.

### 12.7 No Heckman Correction for CEO Selection

The probit supplement documents that CEO absence is non-random, but no Heckman two-stage correction or inverse-probability weighting is applied to the primary estimation. The selection concern is disclosed but not remediated.

### 12.8 MAJOR: Summary Statistics Computed on Wrong Sample (RT2-1)

**Status:** OPEN -- must be resolved before thesis submission.

`make_summary_stats_table` is called on the Main-filtered `panel` (`run_h7_illiquidity.py` L408-413) BEFORE the complete-case and min-calls filters are applied (which happen inside `prepare_regression_data` at L416-419). This means:

1. The summary statistics table includes observations with missing IVs, missing controls, and missing DV values.
2. The summary statistics do NOT represent the actual regression sample.
3. Firms excluded by the min-calls filter (MIN_CALLS_PER_FIRM=5) are included in the summary statistics.
4. The N in the summary statistics table will be larger than the N in the regression table, which is confusing and potentially misleading to reviewers.

This is a standard empirical-methods error -- summary statistics should describe the regression sample, not the pre-filter sample.

**Recommended fix:** Move `make_summary_stats_table` inside the regression loop (after `prepare_regression_data`) or compute it on the col-1 regression sample (the most restrictive base-controls sample).

## 14. Design Decisions & Rationale

| # | Decision | Rationale | Code reference |
|---|----------|-----------|----------------|
| D1 | 4 simultaneous IVs (CEO/Manager x QA/Pres) | Tests whether the uncertainty-illiquidity channel operates through specific speaker roles or call segments, rather than testing one IV at a time. Matches design across all hypothesis suites. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L66-70 |
| D2 | Mirrors H14 (bid-ask spread) pattern | H14 and H7 both test microstructure outcomes (illiquidity, spreads) using event-window DVs. Sharing the same control set, FE structure, and lagged-DV approach ensures within-thesis consistency. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L72 comment |
| D3 | pre_call_amihud as lagged-DV control | Absorbs baseline firm-level illiquidity variation not captured by firm FE. Analogous to H14's PreCallSpread. Standard in event-study designs. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L81 |
| D4 | Extended controls include Volatility, StockPrice, Turnover | Microstructure variables that are known determinants of illiquidity. Their inclusion in the extended (but not base) specification allows assessment of robustness to microstructure confounders. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L84-89 |
| D5 | Fiscal year (fyearq_int) as time FE, not call quarter | Consistency with all other suites. H14 (also event-window DV) uses fyearq_int, establishing precedent for event-window suites using fiscal year rather than calendar quarter. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L220 |
| D6 | MIN_CALLS_PER_FIRM = 5 | Ensures sufficient within-firm variation for firm-FE estimation. Standard threshold across suites. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L98 |
| D7 | Main sample only (excludes FF12 8, 11) | Finance and utility firms have structurally different liquidity profiles. Excluding them avoids conflating regulatory/structural differences with the uncertainty effect. | `run_h7_illiquidity.py` L163 |
| D8 | Window size w=3 trading days | Balance between capturing the immediate post-call illiquidity response and avoiding contamination from subsequent information events. Configurable via AmihudChangeBuilder config. | `amihud_change.py` L49 |
| D9 | Call-day exclusion from both windows | Conservative: avoids ambiguity about intraday timing of the call relative to market close. Standard in event-study literature. | `amihud_change.py` L327-328 |
| D10 | Dropped from old H7: Entire_All_Negative_pct, StockReturn, amihud_change_w5, AmihudIlliq (inter-call level) | Redundant with 4 simultaneous IVs (Entire_All_Negative_pct), or superseded by the event-window DV design (AmihudIlliq level, alternative window width). | Prior version comparison |
| D11 | CEO Presence Probit as standalone supplement | Selection characterisation is analytically distinct from the primary hypothesis test. Keeping it as a separate script with its own outputs allows independent review and prevents entangling selection diagnostics with the main regression pipeline. | `ceo_presence_probit.py` |
