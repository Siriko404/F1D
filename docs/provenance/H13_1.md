### A.2 Model Family

**Model specification (per moderator):**

```
DV = b1 * Mgr_QA_Unc_c + b2 * z(log(MOD)) + b3 * (Mgr_QA_Unc_c x z(log(MOD)))
     + controls [+ CapexAt_t for lead specs]
     + IndustryFE(FF12) + FiscalYearFE + e
```

- `b3` is the coefficient of interest: does market structure moderate the effect of managerial Q&A uncertainty on capital expenditure?
- `b1` represents the marginal effect of uncertainty when the moderator is at its sample mean (because the moderator is z-scored)
- `b2` represents the marginal effect of market structure when the IV is at its sample mean (because the IV is mean-centered)

### A.6 Model-Family Addendum: Redesign Rationale

The old H13.1 had critical issues documented in the archived provenance:

1. **Uncentered interactions** -- raw product of IV x HHI; b1 and b2 uninterpretable at meaningful data points
2. **HHI-only** -- TNIC3TSIMM (available in the same data file) was not used
3. **16 models** -- 4 IVs x 2 DVs x 2 FE types, one IV per model
4. **1/16 significant** (p = 0.027) -- did not survive Bonferroni correction (threshold = 0.003)
5. **TNIC year-key mismatch** -- merged on `fyearq_int` instead of calendar year of datadate

The redesign addresses all five:
1. IV mean-centered; moderators log-transformed then z-scored on Main sample
2. TSIMM is primary moderator; HHI is robustness check
3. 4 models only (1 IV x 2 DVs x 2 moderators); single IV chosen based on H13 results
4. 2/4 models significant for TSIMM (both p < 0.025); HHI models null (2/4)
5. TNIC year-key mismatch is **still present** (see Known Issue #1 -- inherited from old design)

## F. Variable Dictionary (Paper-grade)

### F.1 Dependent Variables

#### F.1.1 `CapexAt` -- Capital Expenditure Intensity (Contemporaneous)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Formula** | `capxy_annual_Q4 / atq_annual_lag1` |
| **Timing** | Fiscal year t (same year as the earnings call) |
| **Code** | `_compustat_engine.py` lines 1079-1085 |

**Construction chain:** (1) Numerator: Q4 `capxy` (YTD-cumulative capital expenditure) extracted via `_compute_annual_q4_variable()`, filtered to `fqtr == 4`, deduplicated by `(gvkey, fyearq)`, joined to all quarters. (2) Denominator: lagged Q4 `atq` via `_compute_annual_q4_variable_lag()`, shifted `fyearq += 1`. (3) Division: `np.where(atq_annual_lag1 > 0, capxy_annual / atq_annual_lag1, np.nan)`. (4) Inf replaced with NaN. (5) Per-year 1%/99% winsorization. (6) Matched to calls via `merge_asof` backward.

**Moulton note:** CapexAt is constant within firm-fiscal-year. All calls in the same `(gvkey, fyearq_int)` receive the identical value.

#### F.1.2 `CapexAt_lead` -- Capital Expenditure Intensity (Forward, t+1)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Formula** | CapexAt for fiscal year t+1, via within-firm shift with consecutive-year validation |
| **Code** | `build_h13_capex_panel.py` lines 215-337 (`create_capex_lead`) |

**Construction chain:** (1) Attach `fyearq` via `merge_asof`. (2) For each `(gvkey, fyearq_int)`, select latest call's CapexAt (inconsequential since CapexAt is firm-year constant). (3) Within-gvkey `shift(-1)` on sorted `fyearq_grp`. (4) Consecutive-year validation: NaN if `fyearq_lead != fyearq_grp + 1`. (5) Merge back to call level by `(gvkey, fyearq_int)`, zero-row-delta enforced.

### F.4 Interaction Terms

| Variable | Formula | Code |

**Centering/standardization protocol:** The IV is mean-centered (not standardized). The moderators are log-transformed then z-scored (mean=0, sd=1). This is a major improvement over the old design (raw product without centering). With this protocol:
- `b1` (IV coefficient) = marginal effect of uncertainty when moderator is at its sample mean (z=0)
- `b2` (moderator coefficient) = marginal effect of market structure when IV is at its sample mean (centered=0)
- `b3` (interaction coefficient) = change in marginal effect of uncertainty per 1-SD increase in log(moderator)

## G. Outliers, Missing Data, Scaling, and Model-Specific Data Rules

### G.1 Log Transform of Moderators

Both moderators are log-transformed before z-scoring. TSIMM has a minimum of 1.0 (so `log(1.0) = 0` is valid). HHI has a minimum of 0.0102 (so `log(0.0102) = -4.585` is valid). No zero or negative values exist in either moderator's raw distribution, so the log transform does not produce NaN or -inf. Verified from Python check of TNIC data.

### G.2 Inf Handling

`df.replace([np.inf, -np.inf], np.nan)` applied at runner line 317, before complete-case filtering. This catches any inf values that might arise from log transforms (though none should occur given the moderator ranges).

### G.3 Missing Data Protocol

- **TNIC unmatched:** Left join produces NaN for unmatched firm-years. These are dropped at complete-case filtering.
- **HHI null (TSIMM present):** 7,592 TNIC rows have TSIMM but no HHI. This explains the 13-call difference between Col 1 (N=75,110) and Col 3 (N=75,097).
- **IV missing:** Calls without Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct (e.g., no manager Q&A turns identified) receive NaN.

### G.4 Winsorization Protocol

- **Compustat controls:** Per-year 1%/99% (engine level)
- **Textual IV:** Per-year 0%/99% upper-only (LinguisticEngine)
- **Moderators:** Not winsorized (z-scoring on log-scale is applied, but no clipping)
- **Interaction terms:** Not separately winsorized
- **Runner level:** No additional winsorization

## J. Known Issues / Ambiguities

### J.1 TNIC Year-Key Mismatch [HIGH SEVERITY -- Inherited]

**Description:** The TNIC `year` field represents `calendar_year(datadate)` (first 4 digits of the YYYYMMDD Compustat `datadate`). The code renames TNIC `year` to `fyearq_int` and merges against the panel's `fyearq_int`, which is the Compustat fiscal year. For firms with non-December fiscal year-ends (~30% of Compustat), `calendar_year(datadate)` can differ from `fyearq` by one year.

**Example:** A firm with January 31 FYE has `fyearq = 2019` for FY2019 but `datadate = 20200131`, so TNIC has `year = 2020`. The merge looks for `fyearq_int = 2019` in TNIC but the correct record is at `year = 2020`.

**Impact:** Affected firms receive either NaN (no match) or wrong-year TNIC values. The 1,398 unmatched TSIMM calls (1.6%) may partially reflect this. More insidiously, some firms may silently receive misaligned TNIC data.

**Evidence:** TNIC readme Technical Note 1; runner lines 212-214.

**Status:** This issue was documented in the old H13.1 provenance and is **NOT fixed in the redesign**.

**Remediation:** Merge on `int(str(datadate)[:4])` instead of `fyearq_int`, or construct a Compustat-to-TNIC crosswalk using `datadate`.

### J.2 Moulton (1990) Pseudoreplication [HIGH SEVERITY -- Inherited from H13]

**Description:** Both DVs (CapexAt, CapexAt_lead) are constant within firm-fiscal-year. Both moderators (TNIC3TSIMM, TNIC3HHI) are firm-year variables, also constant within firm-fiscal-year. The interaction term is therefore also constant within firm-fiscal-year (centered IV varies, but the moderator and DV do not). The regression treats each call as an independent observation, but for approximately half the RHS (moderator, interaction with firm-year-constant component, DV), the effective sample size is the number of firm-years, not calls.

**Mitigation:** Firm-clustered SEs correct for within-firm serial correlation but do not specifically address within-firm-year pseudoreplication.

**Firm-year-collapsed check:** Not implemented. This would involve averaging the IV across calls within each firm-year, then running the regression at the firm-year level.

### J.3 Contemporaneous Moderator [MODERATE SEVERITY]

**Description:** TNIC3 measures are merged on the same fiscal year as the DV. If investment decisions causally affect competitive positioning (or vice versa), the moderator is endogenous. The TNIC readme explicitly states: "The data contained here is not lagged. Researchers needing lagged data must lag the data on their own." No lagged-TNIC specification is included.

### J.4 No Firm FE Specifications [MODERATE SEVERITY -- Design Choice]

**Description:** The redesign uses Industry(FF12) + FiscalYear FE only, dropping the Firm FE specifications present in the old design and in the parent H13. This is a deliberate choice: the parent H13 found that significance disappeared under Firm FE for all IVs, suggesting that between-firm variation drives the results. The interaction test inherits this limitation.

**Counterargument:** Dropping Firm FE means the moderator's effect includes between-firm variation in TNIC measures that may correlate with omitted firm characteristics. A firm FE specification would test whether within-firm temporal variation in TNIC moderates the within-firm uncertainty-capex relationship.

### J.5 TSIMM and HHI Are Not Independent Tests [MODERATE SEVERITY]

**Description:** Corr(z(log(HHI)), z(log(TSIMM))) = -0.70. The two panels cannot be interpreted as independent replications. A firm with high TSIMM almost certainly has low HHI. The TSIMM interaction is positive and significant; the HHI interaction is negative and null. The sign pattern is consistent with the correlation structure (if TSIMM amplifies, HHI should dampen, and vice versa), but the significance asymmetry could reflect statistical power differences or genuine economic differences between the two constructs.

### J.6 Within-R-squared Very Low in Contemporaneous Specs [LOW-MODERATE SEVERITY]

**Description:** Col 1 (CapexAt, TSIMM) has within-R-squared of 0.0083 and Col 3 (CapexAt, HHI) has 0.0015. These are extremely low, meaning the models explain less than 1% of within-group (industry x year) variation in CapexAt. The lead specs (Cols 2, 4) have much higher within-R-squared (0.073, 0.069) because the lagged DV (CapexAt) dominates the model.

**Interpretation:** The low R-squared in contemporaneous specs suggests the uncertainty-capex relationship is economically small relative to the total within-group variation in capex intensity. The high R-squared in lead specs is driven almost entirely by the lagged DV (b = 0.688-0.691), not by the IV or interaction.

### J.7 No Multiple Testing Correction [LOW SEVERITY -- Improvement from Old Design]

**Description:** 4 hypothesis tests are conducted (vs. 16 in the old design). Bonferroni threshold = 0.05/4 = 0.0125. TSIMM interactions have p = 0.023 and p = 0.019, which do NOT survive Bonferroni. However, this is less severe than the old design because: (a) only 4 tests vs. 16, (b) the two TSIMM tests are conceptually one hypothesis (same moderator, two DVs), (c) the two HHI tests are a planned robustness check.

If treating TSIMM as the single primary moderator (2 tests), Bonferroni threshold = 0.025, and both pass (p=0.023, p=0.019). This is a defensible framing.

### J.8 LaTeX Table Note Misstatement [COSMETIC]

**Description:** The LaTeX table note states "Mgr QA Uncertainty mean-centered; coefficient = effect at sample-mean uncertainty." This is reversed: because the IV is centered, the **moderator** coefficient (b2) represents the effect at sample-mean uncertainty, and the **IV** coefficient (b1) represents the effect at sample-mean moderator (z=0). The note as written describes b1's interpretation, not the centering benefit.

### J.9 No Economic Magnitude Discussion [LOW-MODERATE SEVERITY]

**Description:** The interaction coefficient for TSIMM (Col 1) is +0.0048. This means a 1-SD increase in log(TSIMM) is associated with a 0.0048 percentage point increase in the marginal effect of uncertainty on CapexAt. Given that CapexAt has mean = 0.0551 and SD = 0.0694, and Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct has SD = 0.3206, the economic magnitude is: a 1-SD increase in both uncertainty and log(TSIMM) is associated with a change of 0.0048 * 0.3206 = 0.0015 in CapexAt, or about 2.7% of the DV mean. This is small but non-trivial. However, no such calculation appears in the code or outputs.

## K. Corporate Finance Thesis Referee Assessment

### K.1 Identification Strategy

**Strengths:**
- Mean-centering the IV and z-scoring the moderators is best practice for interaction models (Aiken & West, 1991; Brambor, Clark & Golder, 2006). This is a major improvement over the old design.
- Using only the one IV that was significant in the parent H13 avoids the multiple-testing explosion of the old design.
- Testing both TSIMM (primary) and HHI (robustness) leverages the same data file but with theoretically distinct constructs.

**Weaknesses:**
- **No causal identification.** The suite estimates conditional correlations, not causal effects. The moderator is contemporaneous with the DV. No instrumental variables, regression discontinuity, or difference-in-differences design is employed.
- **No Firm FE.** The omission of Firm FE is deliberate but creates a vulnerability: a referee can argue that between-firm variation in TNIC measures is confounded with omitted firm characteristics.
- **TNIC year-key mismatch** introduces measurement error in the moderator. If this error is classical (random), it biases the interaction toward zero (attenuating the finding). If systematic (non-December FYE firms are different), it could bias in either direction.

### K.2 Statistical Inference

**Strengths:**
- Firm-clustered SEs are appropriate for the call-level panel.
- Two-tailed tests are conservative and appropriate given tentative directional predictions.
- The interaction p-values (0.023, 0.019) are at the borderline of conventional significance but consistent across both the contemporaneous and lead DV.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Moulton problem.** The DV and moderator are both firm-year level, but estimation is at the call level. The interaction term's variation is partly call-level (via centered IV) and partly firm-year-level (via z-scored moderator). Firm-clustered SEs may not fully correct for the within-firm-year inflation.
- **Borderline significance.** Both TSIMM interaction p-values are between 0.018 and 0.024. A referee who applies Bonferroni over all 4 tests (threshold = 0.0125) would reject both. The defense relies on framing TSIMM as the sole primary moderator.
- **No two-way clustering.** No firm + fiscal-year double-clustered SEs are provided. Given that TNIC measures have substantial year-level variation (market structures evolve over time), year-clustering could matter.

### K.3 Robustness

**What is present:**
- Two moderators (TSIMM primary, HHI robustness)
- Two DVs (contemporaneous and lead)
- Lagged DV control in lead specs
- Extended controls (11 variables)

**What is absent:**
- No Firm FE specifications
- No lagged moderator (TNIC_{t-1})
- No firm-year-collapsed estimation
- No double-clustered SEs (firm + year)
- No alternative TNIC cutoffs (TNIC-4, TNIC-5)
- No split-sample analysis (high vs. low moderator subsamples)
- No placebo or falsification tests
- No examination of moderator persistence / within-firm variation
- No bootstrap inference as sensitivity check on clustered SEs

### K.4 Variable Construction

**Strengths:**
- DV construction (CapexAt) follows standard practice: Q4 capxy / lagged total assets.
- Lead variable has consecutive-year validation.
- Zero-row-delta guards on all merges prevent silent data quality issues.
- Moderator transformation (log + z-score) is appropriate for right-skewed distributions.

**Weaknesses:**
- TobinsQ formula diverges from textbook (inherited from H13; see `docs/provenance/H13.md` L03).
- `RD_Intensity = xrdq.fillna(0) / atq` treats missing R&D as zero.
- TNIC moderators are not winsorized; extreme values (TSIMM up to 143) may drive results after log-transform (though z-scoring bounds influence to the range of the z-distribution).

### K.5 Interpretation

**The positive TSIMM interaction is economically interpretable:** In product markets with higher similarity (more competitive pressure from substitutes), the positive association between managerial uncertainty language and capital expenditure is amplified. This is consistent with the "strategic investment response" channel: firms in more competitive markets with uncertain managers invest more aggressively (possibly defensively).

**The null HHI interaction is informative:** Market concentration does not significantly moderate the uncertainty-capex relationship. This could mean: (a) concentration per se is less relevant than product similarity for the investment channel, (b) the high negative correlation with TSIMM (-0.70) means HHI largely captures the inverse of the same construct, (c) the HHI interaction has the wrong functional form (linear in z-score may not capture nonlinear concentration effects).

**Caution on causal language:** The coefficient of interest (b3) measures a cross-sectional association, not a causal effect. The paper should not claim that product similarity "causes" the uncertainty-capex effect to strengthen.

## N. Final Readiness Statement

**Suite H13.1 (Redesigned) is a substantial improvement over the original.** The mean-centered IV, z-scored moderators, focused 4-model design, and inclusion of both TSIMM (primary) and HHI (robustness) address all major methodological weaknesses of the old version. **Recommendation:** Fix L01 and L02 before circulating the thesis chapter. The remaining issues (L03-L13) are standard referee improvements that can be addressed in revision.
