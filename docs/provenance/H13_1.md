## Design

H13 found that 4 of 6 uncertainty IVs significantly predict CapexAt under Industry FE, but none survive under Firm FE. H13.1 tests whether **product market competition** moderates this relationship using Hoberg-Phillips TNIC3HHI.

Each IV gets its own regression with its own interaction term (one IV per model):

```
CapexAt = b1*IV_k + b2*tnic3hhi + b3*(IV_k x tnic3hhi) + controls + FE + e
```

b3 (interaction) is the coefficient of interest.

## Competition Measure

**TNIC3HHI** (Hoberg & Phillips, *Journal of Political Economy*, 2016):
- Firm-specific text-based Herfindahl index from 10-K business descriptions
- High HHI = concentrated market / monopolist; Low HHI = fragmented / competitive
- Firm-year level, merged to call-level panel on (gvkey, fyearq_int)
- Coverage: 98.3% of calls (111,000 / 112,968)
- Within-industry variation: 85.7% (no absorption risk under Industry FE)
- Within-firm variation: 27.8% (b2 has inflated SEs under Firm FE; b3 well-identified via call-level IV variation)

## Specification

- **24 models:** 6 IVs x 2 DVs (CapexAt, CapexAt_lead) x 2 FE (Industry, Firm)
- **Controls:** Extended (Size, TobinsQ, ROA, Lev, CashHoldings, DividendPayer, OCF_Volatility, SalesGrowth, RD_Intensity, CashFlow, Volatility)
- **Sample:** Main only (FF12 not in {8, 11})
- **Hypothesis:** Two-tailed on interaction (b3 != 0)
- **SEs:** Firm-clustered
- **MIN_CALLS_PER_FIRM:** 5

## Key Results

1/24 significant interactions at p<0.05:
- Col 7: CEO_QA_Uncertainty x HHI under Firm FE, DV=CapexAt: b3=-0.0063, p=0.027

The interaction is negative: in more concentrated markets (high HHI), CEO Q&A uncertainty has a weaker (more negative) effect on capex. This suggests competitive pressure amplifies the uncertainty-investment link, but only for CEO Q&A under Firm FE.
