# 2) The main problem: M2 fails a decisive falsification test

Your placebo (“future CEO uncertainty / Lead1”) is **strongly positive and significant** in all samples. 
You correctly label this as “FAILED.” 

### What that means (as a referee)

With Call FE + Speaker FE, a lead being significant implies at least one of:

* **Within-call time-varying confounding** (latent “call uncertainty climate” evolving during the call),
* **Feedback** (manager uncertainty influences subsequent CEO uncertainty),
* **Shared shock structure** (both respond to the same analyst/question/topic sequence),
* or **mechanical construction issues** (see Section 3).

So **M2 cannot be presented as transmission/influence**. The *best* defensible statement is exactly what your updated memo says: a within-call temporal association. 

---

# 3) Critical data-integrity issue: row counts imply a merge “explosion”

Table 6 says:

* Total manager turns (Main) = **1,485,219**
* Turns with prior CEO unc = **1,223,289**
* Valid M2 obs = **1,223,289**
* **Final M2 obs after merging controls = 1,699,402** 

This is not a small discrepancy. **Merging controls should not increase the number of turn observations** unless:

* your “turn” unit is not uniquely keyed, or
* a merge became **many-to-many**, duplicating rows (e.g., Compustat/IBES join not uniquely matched to call/turn), or
* you are inadvertently appending/stacking panels.

### Why this matters

A many-to-many merge can:

* inflate t-stats,
* distort the placebo test,
* and even change the *sign* depending on duplicated subsets.

### What I would require before I trust *any* M2 result

You need to demonstrate that **each turn is uniquely identified** and merges are validated:

* Define a unique key, e.g. `(gvkey, file_name, turn_index, speaker_id)` and prove `duplicated(key)==0` pre/post merge.
* Enforce pandas merge validation (`validate="one_to_one"` or `one_to_many"` as appropriate).
* Show a reconciliation table where **row counts are invariant** after adding controls.

Until this is fixed, M2 is not audit-clean.

# 5) M1 (call-level): plausible, but still “association” unless you tighten the estimand

M1 Main is positive and significant (β ≈ 0.0173, SE 0.0037). 
This is your strongest “predetermined regressor” design.

However, as a referee I still worry about:

* **CEO–CFO matching/selection** (CEOs choose CFOs; CFO style may co-move),
* remaining time-varying firm shocks even with YQ FE.

What would strengthen M1 materially:

* Add **CFO fixed effects** (or CFO ID controls) if available,
* show robustness to **CEO turnover events** (difference-in-differences around CEO change),
* report effect size in SD units of CFO uncertainty (not just IHS).
