## SECTION A — MODERATOR FIT MEMO

I treated the final LaTeX results surface as the authority for eligible targets, the suite packets as the authority for unit/timing/FE interpretation, and the TNIC readme as the authority for TNIC construction: firm-year data keyed by `gvkey` and Compustat-matched year, not lagged by default, `TNIC3HHI` includes the focal firm, and `TNIC3HHI` can be missing when `TNIC3TSIMM` is present. 

### 1) H1_F1_mgr_pres_cash

* **finding_id:** `H1_F1_mgr_pres_cash`
* **restated_finding:** `Manager_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` is the carried H1 signal: higher manager presentation uncertainty predicts higher `CashHoldings` and higher `CashHoldings_lead`, but only in Industry+FiscalYear specifications.
* **variation_signature:** primarily **between-firm**; call-level IV with annual firm-year cash outcome repeated across calls within the same firm-year; **contemporaneous + lead**; **speaker-specific** and **section-specific**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** passes the eligibility gate, but only as a **secondary** moderation idea. A slow-moving annual market-structure layer is compatible with a between-firm cash result. The best economic channel is that product-space structure changes how much uncertain presentation language maps into precautionary liquidity policy.
* **HHI_fit:** only partial. Concentration/market power can matter for cash policy, but here `TNIC3HHI` risks mostly re-labeling cross-sectional differences in cash-generation capacity or market power rather than adding new mechanism.
* **TSIMM_fit:** better. `TNIC3TSIMM` can speak to differentiation vs. crowded product space; that is a more direct channel for why uncertainty in prepared remarks might map into stronger or weaker precautionary cash buffering.
* **timing_alignment_note:** use `TNIC_t` for `CashHoldings_t`. For `CashHoldings_{t+1}`, `TNIC_t` is coherent only if explicitly framed as the call-year market environment; a lagged moderator requires an **explicitly constructed** `TNIC_{t-1}`.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** good under the parent Industry+FY design; weak as a first-pass firm-FE moderation because the parent effect disappears there.
* **missingness_risk_note:** start with `TNIC3TSIMM`; `TNIC3HHI` can drop observations that still have TSIMM.
* **final_use_rank:** **plausible but secondary moderator candidate**. 

### 2) H1_F2_mgr_qa_cash

* **finding_id:** `H1_F2_mgr_qa_cash`
* **restated_finding:** `Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct` predicts higher contemporaneous `CashHoldings`, but only in Firm+FiscalYear specifications.
* **variation_signature:** primarily **within-firm**; **contemporaneous**; call-level IV against an annual-ish cash outcome repeated within firm-quarter; **speaker-specific** and **section-specific**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** weak. The parent finding only survives under firm FE, while TNIC is an annual firm-year moderator that is likely too slow-moving to be a natural first interaction for this signature.
* **HHI_fit:** weak.
* **TSIMM_fit:** slightly better conceptually than HHI, but still weak because the core problem is FE/variation fit, not story fit.
* **timing_alignment_note:** any interaction would have to use the same firm-year TNIC value repeated across calls in that year; lagging would make within-firm movement even thinner.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** poor. A firm-FE moderation test asks annual TNIC to carry enough within-firm time variation to explain a finding that already lives only in within-firm variation.
* **missingness_risk_note:** `TNIC3HHI` would add avoidable sample loss on top of an already demanding firm-FE design.
* **final_use_rank:** **better as a control / stratifier / descriptive context than as a moderator**. 

### 3) H2_F1_mgr_qa_invest

* **finding_id:** `H2_F1_mgr_qa_invest`
* **restated_finding:** the only H2 signal worth even screening is `Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct` in industry-FE investment-efficiency specifications, but the H2 packet itself characterizes the suite as overwhelmingly null.
* **variation_signature:** mostly **between-firm**; **contemporaneous**; call-level IV against an **annual firm-year DV** repeated across calls; outcome-specific to abnormal investment.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** fails the economic-content screen in practice. Unit and timing can be aligned, but the parent result is barely above false-positive expectation and sits inside a suite the packet itself says is consistent with null.
* **HHI_fit:** not compelling.
* **TSIMM_fit:** not compelling.
* **timing_alignment_note:** `TNIC_t` could be aligned, but alignment alone does not rescue a weak target.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** mechanically acceptable under Industry+FY, substantively unpersuasive.
* **missingness_risk_note:** extra HHI missingness would further weaken an already marginal use case.
* **final_use_rank:** **not a useful addition for this finding**. 

### 4) H4_F1_ceo_pres_booklev

* **finding_id:** `H4_F1_ceo_pres_booklev`
* **restated_finding:** on the final results surface, `CEO_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` is the recurring H4 BookLev signal: positive in Industry+FiscalYear BookLev specifications, both contemporaneously and one year forward.
* **variation_signature:** mainly **between-firm**; **outcome-specific** to leverage; call-level IV with slow-moving accounting outcome; **contemporaneous + lead**; **section-specific**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** this is one of the cleanest TNIC moderation opportunities. Market structure is a natural layer for explaining why uncertain presentation language translates into more or less leverage capacity.
* **HHI_fit:** strongest. `TNIC3HHI` captures focal-firm-inclusive concentration / market-power environment and maps directly to debt capacity, pricing power, and financing tolerance under uncertainty.
* **TSIMM_fit:** plausible but secondary. Product similarity/differentiation can reroute financing responses, but the channel is less direct than with HHI.
* **timing_alignment_note:** use `TNIC_t` for `BookLev_t`. For `BookLev_{t+1}`, prefer an **explicitly constructed** lag if the moderator is meant to be predetermined; do not assume lagging.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** strong in the parent Industry+FY structure; not natural to force firm-FE moderation when the parent effect is not there.
* **missingness_risk_note:** compare HHI and TSIMM on common support; HHI may shrink the estimation sample.
* **final_use_rank:** **strong moderator candidate**. The design is analytically strong, although the H4 packet itself notes that its own audit did not verify a fresh run output, so this should stay tied to the final results surface rather than over-sold as packet-verified output. 

### 5) H4_F2_ceo_pres_debtcap

* **finding_id:** `H4_F2_ceo_pres_debtcap`
* **restated_finding:** on the final results surface, `CEO_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` is also the recurring H4 DebtToCapital signal: positive in Industry+FiscalYear DebtToCapital specifications, contemporaneously and at t+1.
* **variation_signature:** mainly **between-firm**; **outcome-specific** to capital-structure composition; call-level IV with slow-moving accounting outcome; **contemporaneous + lead**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** also a strong pass. If TNIC is useful anywhere in H4, it is here: product-market structure can plausibly condition whether uncertainty maps into more debt-heavy financing.
* **HHI_fit:** strongest.
* **TSIMM_fit:** plausible secondary.
* **timing_alignment_note:** same logic as BookLev: `TNIC_t` for the contemporaneous DV; explicit lag if the lead interaction is meant to be predetermined.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** strongest in cross-sectional Industry+FY logic.
* **missingness_risk_note:** same HHI-vs-TSIMM support issue as above.
* **final_use_rank:** **strong moderator candidate**. 

### 6) H5_F1_ceo_pres_dispersion

* **finding_id:** `H5_F1_ceo_pres_dispersion`
* **restated_finding:** on the final results surface, `CEO_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` is the only H5 signal worth carrying forward for post-call analyst dispersion.
* **variation_signature:** **call-level**; very short-horizon **post-call** outcome; mixed between-/within-firm signature; **speaker-specific** and **section-specific**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** partial pass only. An annual firm-year moderator can be interpreted as a background environment, but this is a fast event-window outcome, so the moderator is necessarily a coarse layer rather than a tightly matched state variable.
* **HHI_fit:** weak to moderate, and indirect.
* **TSIMM_fit:** better. Similarity/differentiation can plausibly amplify how uncertain presentations widen analyst disagreement, especially through peer-comparison noise.
* **timing_alignment_note:** `TNIC_t` matched to the fiscal year containing the call is the coherent choice; lagging is optional, not automatic.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** more credible in Industry+FY than in firm FE because annual TNIC is slow-moving.
* **missingness_risk_note:** TSIMM should be the first pass; HHI risks sample loss for a design that is already call-window specific.
* **final_use_rank:** **plausible but secondary moderator candidate**. Procedurally, this remains weaker than H1/H4 because the H5 packet documents the design but does not verify an executed run output. 

### 7) H12_F1_avg_ceo_pres_payout_lead

* **finding_id:** `H12_F1_avg_ceo_pres_payout_lead`
* **restated_finding:** on the final results surface, `Avg_CEO_Pres_Uncertainty_pct` enters significantly only in firm-FE lead payout specifications, and in the positive direction rather than the original payout-reduction direction.
* **variation_signature:** exact **firm-year** unit match; **lead/predictive**; primarily **within-firm**; annual averaged IV.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** unit alignment is perfect, but moderation value is low. This is a within-firm, lead-only finding, and the most plausible TNIC channels risk becoming post-hoc reinterpretations rather than genuine moderator content.
* **HHI_fit:** limited.
* **TSIMM_fit:** weaker.
* **timing_alignment_note:** if ever tested, use an **explicitly lagged** TNIC for the lead payout design.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** poor as a first-pass moderator because annual TNIC may not move enough within firm under firm FE.
* **missingness_risk_note:** HHI missingness would bite without offsetting conceptual gain.
* **final_use_rank:** **better as a control / stratifier / descriptive context than as a moderator**. 

### 8) H13_F1_mgr_qa_capex

* **finding_id:** `H13_F1_mgr_qa_capex`
* **restated_finding:** on the final results surface, `Manager_QA_Uncertainty_pct` is the recurring H13 signal: positive in Industry+FiscalYear `CapexAt` and `CapexAt_lead` specifications.
* **variation_signature:** mainly **between-firm**; call-level IV against annual firm-year capex intensity repeated across calls; **contemporaneous + lead**; **speaker-specific** and **section-specific**.
* **TNIC_fit_assessment:** strong pass. Strategic investment is exactly where a product-space moderator can add economic content rather than merely relabeling the result.
* **HHI_fit:** plausible but secondary. Concentration can reduce or redirect strategic investment pressure under uncertainty.
* **TSIMM_fit:** strongest. Total product similarity is the most direct market-structure layer for explaining how uncertainty maps into strategic investment intensity.
* **timing_alignment_note:** use `TNIC_t` for `CapexAt_t`; for `CapexAt_{t+1}`, explicit lagging is cleaner if the moderator is meant to be predetermined.
* **FE_compatibility_note:** strong in the parent Industry+FY design; no need to force firm-FE moderation first.
* **missingness_risk_note:** HHI-vs-TSIMM sample-overlap reporting is essential.
* **final_use_rank:** **strong moderator candidate**. The H13 packet carries only a mild staleness caveat, so this is usable but not as cleanly packet-verified as H1.
