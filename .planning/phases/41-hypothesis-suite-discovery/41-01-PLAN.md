<objective>
Conduct a systematic literature review using PRISMA 2020 methodology to identify all empirical tests of hypotheses linking managerial speech (uncertainty, vagueness, hedging, tone, complexity) to corporate outcomes. Document the evidence base, identify research gaps (hypotheses not yet tested), and produce a prioritized list of novel, theoretically-motivated hypotheses.

Purpose: Phase 41 is a discovery phase that will define the next set of hypotheses to test after H5 (Analyst Dispersion). Without understanding what has already been tested, we risk conducting research that adds no new knowledge.

Output: (1) Literature review summary documenting the state of knowledge, (2) PRISMA flow diagram showing search process, (3) Evidence matrix mapping studies to variables and findings, (4) Ranked list of novel hypotheses with theoretical motivation
</objective>

Search the literature using PRISMA 2020 methodology:

1. **Search Databases** (document each):
   - Web of Science, Scopus, ABI/INFORM Complete, SSRN, Google Scholar
   - Search timeframe: 2010-2025 (post-Loughran-McDonald 2011 dictionary)
   - Record date searched, search string, and results count for each

2. **Search String** (Boolean):
   ```
   ("managerial communication" OR "CEO speech" OR "executive language" OR
    "earnings call*" OR "conference call*" OR "management disclosure*")
   AND
   ("textual analysis" OR "linguistic analysis" OR "content analysis" OR
    "sentiment analysis" OR uncertainty OR vagueness OR tone OR
    readability OR complexity OR "weak modal*" OR hedging)
   AND
   ("corporate governance" OR "financial polic*" OR investment OR
    financing OR payout OR "M&A" OR acquisition OR takeover OR
    "CEO turnover" OR "executive compensation" OR "analyst forecast*" OR
    "stock return*" OR volatil*)
   ```

3. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Empirical archival study of public firms
   - Textual analysis of corporate communication (earnings calls preferred)
   - Tests hypothesis about corporate outcome
   - Published 2010-2025 in peer-reviewed journal or cited working paper

4. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Purely theoretical papers
   - Non-corporate settings (political speech, etc.)
   - Duplicate datasets (prefer most recent)

5. **Document PRISMA Flow**:
   - Identification: Total results from each database
   - Screening: Duplicates removed, title/abstract exclusions with reasons
   - Eligibility: Full-text reviewed, excluded with reasons
   - Inclusion: Final count of studies
