```markdown
### H6: Managerial Hedging and M&A Targeting

**Theoretical Rationale:**
Hedging language (weak modals: may/might/could) in earnings calls may signal strategic ambiguity or undervaluation, attracting acquirers who see opportunity, or may indicate managerial caution that reduces takeover attractiveness.

**Formal Hypothesis:**
H6a: Higher hedging language in earnings calls increases the likelihood of becoming an M&A target.
H6b: If targeted, higher hedging language is associated with lower deal premiums (acquirers discount for uncertainty).

**Independent Variable:**
- Primary: Manager_QA_Weak_Modal_pct (from Step 2)
- Alternative: CEO_QA_Weak_Modal_pct
- Timing: Speech_t (quarter before M&A announcement)

**Dependent Variable:**
- H6a: M&A target dummy (1 if firm announces M&A in quarter t+1, else 0)
- H6b: Deal premium = (Offer price - Price 1 day prior) / Price 1 day prior
- Data source: sdc-ma-merged.parquet

**Control Variables:**
- Firm controls: Size, Tobin's Q, Leverage, ROA, Cash holdings
- Governance: G-index (if available), Board independence
- Market: Abnormal returns, Volatility
- Industry-year fixed effects

**Sample:**
- Source: Merge earnings calls + SDC M&A + Compustat
- Expected N: ~25,000 firm-years
- Events: ~1,000s of M&A announcements

**Methodology:**
- H6a: Logistic regression with firm + year + industry FE
- H6b: OLS regression on deal sample with controls
- SE: Clustered at firm level

**Predicted Direction:**
- H6a: beta > 0 (hedging increases targeting likelihood)
- H6b: beta < 0 (hedging reduces deal premium)
```
