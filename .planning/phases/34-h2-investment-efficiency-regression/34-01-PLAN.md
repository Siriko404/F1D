## Hypotheses

**H2a**: β1 < 0 - Higher uncertainty leads to LOWER investment efficiency
**H2b**: β3 > 0 - Leverage attenuates the negative uncertainty-efficiency relationship

(Note: Opposite signs from H1 because H2 is about efficiency loss, not precautionary behavior)

## Model Specifications

Same 4 specs as H1:
1. **primary**: Entity + Year FE, clustered SE at firm level
2. **pooled**: No FE, heteroskedasticity-robust SE
3. **year_only**: Year FE only, clustered SE at firm level
4. **double_cluster**: Entity + Year FE, double-clustered SE (firm + year)

## Dependent Variables

1. **efficiency_score** (primary): 1 - (% overinvestment years + % underinvestment years)
   - Range [0, 1], higher = more efficient
   - Expect: negative β1 (vagueness → lower score)
   
2. **roa_residual** (alternative): Residual from Biddle et al. (2009) regression
   - Cross-sectional OLS residual by industry-year
   - Expect: negative β1 (vaguence → worse ROA performance)
