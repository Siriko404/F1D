## One-page proposal (correct DWZ-style: CEO **fixed effects**, no averages)

### Title

**Tone-at-the-Top in Uncertainty:** Does a CEO’s persistent uncertainty style explain (and transmit to) non-CEO managers’ uncertainty in earnings calls?

### One-sentence contribution

Using the **Dzieliński–Wagner–Zeckhauser (DWZ)** approach, I estimate each CEO’s **persistent uncertainty style as a CEO fixed effect** from CEO Q&A language (controlling for presentation- and question-level uncertainty), then test how much that **CEO fixed-effect style** explains the uncertainty language of **non-CEO executives**—with a **no-lookahead (“real time”)** style estimate and a within-Q&A mechanism test.

---

## Research question

1. **Explained-variance question:** How much of non-CEO management’s uncertainty speech is explained by the CEO’s **persistent uncertainty style** (CEO FE), net of firm conditions and question wording?
2. **Mechanism question:** Is the association consistent with **within-call accommodation/transmission** (non-CEO language shifts toward CEO style within the same analyst question), rather than common firm shocks?

---

## Data & unit of observation

* Earnings call transcripts with **speaker labels** and separation into **Presentation vs Q&A** and ideally **analyst question blocks**.
* Primary unit(s):

  * Stage 1: **CEO × call-quarter** (CEO Q&A answers)
  * Stage 2: **non-CEO exec × call-quarter** (or exec × question)

---

## Key variables (DWZ definitions, then adapted)

### CEO uncertainty in answers (style-bearing object)

* (UncAns^{CEO}_{i,t}): fraction of uncertainty words in **CEO answers** (Q&A) in quarter (t). ([NBER][1])

### Controls capturing “firm/time uncertainty” and question wording

* (UncPre^{CEO}_{i,t}): fraction of uncertainty words in the **CEO presentation** (prepared remarks). 
* (UncQue_{i,t}): fraction of uncertainty words in **analyst questions** on that call. 
* Optional firm characteristics (FirmChars_{i,t}) and time FE (DWZ include these classes of controls). 

### Non-CEO outcome

* (UncAns^{nonCEO}_{f,t}): fraction of uncertainty words in **non-CEO answers** (aggregate or by role: CFO/COO/IR) in firm (f), quarter (t).

---

## Estimation plan

### Stage 1 — Estimate CEO persistent uncertainty style as a **CEO fixed effect** (DWZ core)

Replicate the DWZ style estimation (their Eq. 4 structure) where the CEO’s persistent component is the coefficient on the CEO indicator (CEO FE): 

[
UncAns^{CEO}*{i,t}
= \alpha + \gamma_i \cdot CEO*{i,t} + \beta_s \cdot Speech_{i,t} + \beta_k \cdot FirmChars_{i,t} + Year_t + \varepsilon_{i,t}
]

* **CEO persistent uncertainty style** = (\gamma_i) (DWZ define “clarity” as (-\gamma_i); you can keep uncertainty-style as (\gamma_i)). 
* In practice, (Speech_{i,t}) includes at minimum (UncPre^{CEO}) and (UncQue), which DWZ motivate as controls for firm conditions and question wording. 

### No-lookahead implementation (critical)

To avoid using future calls to estimate (\gamma_i), construct a **real-time** style estimate:

* For each quarter (t), estimate the Stage-1 regression using only calls dated (\le t-1).
* Extract (\widehat{\gamma}_{i,t-1}).
* Require a minimum history (e.g., ≥ 5 prior calls, matching DWZ’s emphasis on sufficient calls for stable estimation). 

This produces (Style^{CEO}*{i}(t-1)=\widehat{\gamma}*{i,t-1}), a time-indexed estimate of a time-invariant trait.

---

### Stage 2 — “How much explained”: does CEO style load into non-CEO uncertainty?

Baseline quarterly test:

[
UncAns^{nonCEO}*{f,t}
= a + b \cdot Style^{CEO}*{CEO(f,t)}(t-1) + \delta_f + \tau_t + X_{f,t}'c + u_{f,t}
]

Deliverables:

* **Incremental / partial (R^2)** from adding CEO style (answering your “how much explained” question).
* Role splits (CFO vs other execs) to show where transmission is strongest.

---

## Mechanism test (stronger, within-call): accommodation inside the same analyst question

If you can parse question blocks in Q&A:

**Unit:** executive (e) × question (k) within a call.

[
Unc_{e,k} = a + \phi\big(Style^{CEO}(t-1)\times CEOFirst_k\big) + \lambda_k + \eta_e + \varepsilon_{e,k}
]

* (\lambda_k): **question fixed effects** (holds topic/content constant)
* (CEOFirst_k): CEO speaks earlier in the answer sequence on that question
* **Key prediction:** (\phi>0) — non-CEO uncertainty increases toward CEO style when the CEO “sets the tone” first.

---

## Hypotheses

* **H1 (explained variance):** Higher real-time CEO uncertainty style (\widehat{\gamma}_{i,t-1}) predicts higher non-CEO uncertainty in the same firm-quarter.
* **H2 (mechanism):** The effect concentrates within Q&A and within-question sequences where CEO speaks earlier.
* **H3 (moderators):** Stronger effects when CEO power is higher (duality, pay slice, founder), and weaker when the CFO is entrenched/high-status.

---

## Falsification / robustness

* **Pre-trends:** no movement in non-CEO uncertainty toward CEO style prior to CEO start date (if turnover design used).
* **Placebos:** run the same pipeline on stylistic features unlikely to transmit (e.g., function-word rates) to test specificity.
* **Control saturation:** verify CEO-style effect survives controlling for (UncPre), (UncQue), firm characteristics, and time FE.

---

## What makes this “novel enough” (vs “just DWZ again”)

DWZ primarily establishes that **managers have persistent styles** and markets react to vagueness; your contribution is the **intra-firm spillover/transmission map**: CEO FE–style → non-CEO speech, quantified as explained variance and pinned down with a within-question accommodation test.
